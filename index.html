Targeted Content 1st Delivery

This clever grid system will re-flow all your content beautifully regardless of the device width, pixel density, or resolution. After all, if content is "King", then why should content be held back from mobile or tablet users. The friendliest grid system makes no enemies. We don't fool users into thinking they are being delivered all the content.

A True One Size Fits All Approach

Our "no worries" frees you, the designer, to spend more time designing and less time worrying about how you website will look on other screens. This grid system is so friendly that it will even work on older phones. You can be sure, that at the very least, every user on every device is able to read your content.

Triple-Level Pro Enhancement

"Enhancement" is the latest and greatest buzzword as of 2014. Simply put, its an approach to design in which the website is tested on the device with the least capabilities, then enhancements are added for more capable machines and browsers. Progressive enhancement ensures that all content will be readable and be seen by all browsers and every devices.

What's In The Box?

Here is a list of the features of the friendliest grid on earth.

  1. 1Responsive

    2. The CSS is responsive and all layout, typography, and images will scale appropriately. All the elements are styled in ems to keep all the padding, borders, margins, typography, and images consistent. This grid system is not dependent on a particular device or the zoom level. The friendliest grid system wishes to be friends with every device or screen resolution. No matter whether your user is viewing your website with a phone, tablet, PC, or HDTV, you can rest easy knowing that your content will be displayed accurately and still retain your design elements.

  2. 2Backwards Compatibility

    3. Many people are not obsessed with owning the newest iPhone or Android phones and still use their older device with an older operating system. Some older phones even lack proper ram and lack the ability to process Javascript. If you using The Friendliest Grid, you need not fret. It is lightweight and will display your content with even the most limited of resources.

  3. 3A Responsive, Touch-Capable Image Slider

    4. Bxslider is an excellent asset to the friendliest grid on earth. It's ability to operate on any touch capable device is unrivaled in other in its class. More information about how to configure Bxslider can be found at the bx slider homepage.

  4. 4Retina & Non-retina Display Support

    5. Ever since the first retina capable iPod, iPad, iPhone, and Apple's retina display on their MacBook Pros, coders and web designers have been searching for a way to serve up images and icons that look crisp and clear. Unfortunately, the only way to do this was to serve up many copies of the same image, which puts a huge overhead on the total weight of the site and slows the load time. Now, through the use of SVG images it is now possible to view the same image at the different resolutions, while maintaining clean, sharp edges consistently.

  5. 5Works on any HD TV

    6. Yep, that's right it will grow to fill even an HDTV set. With some well planned custom media queries any website designed with the friendliest grid system will be easy to read even on an HDTV from your living room couch.

  6. 6Easy to remember class names.

    7. For coders who are planning to implement their site, this is the best news of all. The decision to make this the friendliest grid was also a decision to make it the easiest grid system to use and make your own. For example, one single column row, has been given the class "row", then nested inside the "row"" is "col." Two columns is "two col", then "three col", and "four col". It's that easy.

  7. 7Uses Font Symbol Library

    8. For anyone who wants to develop their website with the current technology, I have included a few fonts from Icomoon. If you take a quick look at the bottom of this page you will see some social media icons. These are not normal pixel or even vector graphics. They are part of a custom font library. This will ensure your symbols stay lean, clean, and crisp. You can go to Icomoon and create your own customizable font set to use for any symbols you may need. The font libraries also come with legacy support for browsers that don't understand the @fontface syntax. My suggestion is to only download what you know you will need. This saves bandwidth overhead which decreases load time.
